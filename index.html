Intro

My name is Hannah Rose White, and I am a student at the College of Wooster. But, isn't there so much more to a person than their name and occupation? Everyone has a past that has shaped their present self, and we all have dreams and aspirations in life. Without fully reflecting as these elements of ourselves, how can we ever understand where we have been, where we are, and where we are going? The goal of this reflection is to answer some of these questions. is to answer some of these questions. In order to give you a boarder understanding of who Hannah Rose White is, I hope I can share parts of my past, my present, and my future.

Past

I am from Parma, Ohio, a suburb located just south of Cleveland. As such, I am an avid Indians, Browns, and Cavs fan and I enjoy going to games downtown. Watching and reading about sports is one of my favorite hobbies. I love spending time outside and particularly enjoy hiking and playing volleyball. I come from a Polish family and a city that has a large Polish and Eastern European population. As a result, I love cooking Polish dishes, practicing the language, and listening to polka. I love photography, especially of nature, and some of my work is included in this reflection piece.

I graduated from Valley Forge High School in 2014. During my time in high school, I was a member of the Student Council, Model United Nations, and the Academic Challenge Team. I was also a member of the Valley Forge Color Guard, a flag and dance team within the marching band. Some of my fondest memories of high school include performing for the halftime crowds and cheering on the football team on Friday nights. I was blessed to have amazing Social Studies teachers who helped me realize my passion for history. As a result, I won the Social Studies Department Award, an honor bestowed by the department on a senior who excelled in that area of study.

Present

Currently, I am a junior at the College of Wooster, a private liberal arts institution in Northeastern Ohio. I am majoring in History with a minor in Art History. Within these areas of study, I am looking to take classes to specialize in Public History. Outside of the classroom, I am the President of Wooster Christian Fellowship, a local chapter of a boarder national organization called InterVarsity Christian Fellowship. Being a part of Wooster Christian Fellowship has been pivotal in my growth as both a Christian and a leader on campus. I have an on-campus job in the History Department as the Department Assistant and as the Assistant to the Administrator Coordinator.

This semester I am working on my Junior Independent Study with Dr. Katherine Holt. During this semester long research project, I am studying the political and corporate rhetoric surrounding Cleveland's Gateway Project of the mid-1990s. The Gateway Project was a downtown redevelopment and stadium construction project that produced both Progressive Field and Quicken Loans Arena. Within this topic, I am also researching how residents of Cleveland and Cuyahoga County responded to the rhetoric and impact of the Gateway Project. Through this study, I hope to show my readers the importance of deconstructing political rhetoric for the benefit of understanding important urban and civic issues.

Future

This summer I will be interning at the Parma Area Historical Society. I will be assisting them in cataloging their collection on a digital platform. During my senior year, I plan to continue my research that I started with my Junior Independent Study. I am looking forward to graduating in May of 2018. After I graduate college, I hope to start my career in Public History by getting a job in one of Cleveland's many museums. I’m excited to continue to live in the Cleveland area, as it is the city that my family has lived in for four generations.

As I speak of future plans, I know that God is laughing to himself. Life never really goes how we expect it, and we often go down paths that we never imagined we’d take. But, I have hope for the future because I know that God will be guiding my steps. Troubles in life do come, and future plans change, but I will never be alone through them. I hope that through this reflective piece, I could illuminate a small, but meaningful, part of who Hannah Rose White is.
